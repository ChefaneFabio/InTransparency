**InTransparency — From Italy-First to Pan-European**
March 2026 | Internal Strategy Document

## Why Europe, Why Now

Three things are converging:

1. **No one owns this space in Europe.** Handshake is US-centric ($300M ARR but almost no EU presence). JobTeaser has 800 EU universities but is a job board — no verification, no portfolios. AlmaLaurea is Italy-only. There is no pan-European verified project marketplace for junior talent.

2. **EU regulation is creating demand.** The Pay Transparency Directive (June 2026) requires objective hiring criteria. The AI Act (August 2026) demands transparent AI in hiring. The Union of Skills strategy pushes cross-border credential recognition. InTransparency is already built for this world.

3. **The infrastructure exists.** The Bologna Process standardized university systems across 49 countries. ECTS credits are universal. Erasmus connects 850+ universities. The technical layer for cross-border talent matching is there — no one has built the marketplace on top of it.

## EU Regulatory Compliance as a Moat

### EU AI Act (August 2026) — Priority 1

Our AI scoring of projects is classified as "high-risk" under the AI Act because it's used in employment decisions.

**Requirements:**
- Transparency: explain why a student got a certain score
- Human oversight: allow institutions to override AI scores
- Data governance: document training data and bias testing
- Risk management: continuous monitoring of scoring accuracy

**Our advantage:** If we comply early, every competitor who doesn't is blocked from the EU market. This is a moat that takes 12-18 months to build — starting now means we're ready before the deadline.

**Actions:**
- Document AI scoring methodology and publish it | Q2 2026
- Implement explainability layer ("This score is based on: code quality 85, innovation 72, complexity 90") | Q2 2026
- Add human override for institutions ("Adjust score" button) | Q3 2026
- Bias testing across gender, nationality, university prestige | Q3 2026

### EU Pay Transparency Directive (June 2026) — Priority 2

Employers must use **objective criteria** in hiring. Self-reported CVs don't qualify. Institution-verified project portfolios with AI scoring do.

**Our pitch to companies:**
"The Pay Transparency Directive requires you to justify hiring decisions with objective criteria. InTransparency provides institution-verified skill assessments — ready-made compliance."

### ESCO Taxonomy Integration — Priority 3

ESCO (European Skills, Competences, and Occupations) is the EU's common skills language — 13,939 skills in 28 languages.

**Why it matters:** If our skill labels map to ESCO, every EU public employment service, every Europass profile, and every EU-funded programme can interoperate with our data.

**Actions:**
- Map our top 200 skills to ESCO codes | Q2 2026
- Auto-suggest ESCO skills when students tag projects | Q3 2026
- Display ESCO codes alongside skills for institutional partners | Q3 2026

### European Digital Credentials (EDC) — Priority 4

EDC allows institutions to issue digitally sealed, verifiable credentials stored in Europass wallets.

**Integration opportunity:** Accept EDC credentials as an additional verification layer. A student with an EDC credential from Polimi + a verified project on InTransparency has double verification.

## The One Thing That Matters

**University partnerships are the moat.** Everything else — the AI, the pricing, the UX — can be copied. But institutional agreements with career services, verified access to academic data, and trust built over semesters of collaboration — that takes years.

Every university we sign in Italy before expanding to DACH is a brick in the wall that Handshake, JobTeaser, or anyone else would need years to replicate in our market.

The Erasmus network is the shortcut. One strong partnership with a university that has 50 Erasmus bilateral agreements gives us warm introductions to 50 universities across Europe.

Start with UniBG. Prove the model. Use the data. Expand.
