## 1. Subject matter and scope

1.1 This DPA governs the processing of personal data carried out by the
Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the use of the
InTransparency platform under the master service agreement (or order
form) signed by the Parties (the "**Main Agreement**").

1.2 In case of conflict between this DPA and the Main Agreement, this DPA
prevails for matters of personal data processing.

1.3 This DPA is concluded pursuant to Article 28 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679
("**GDPR**") and Italian Legislative Decree 196/2003 as amended ("**Codice
Privacy**").

---

## 2. Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined herein have the meaning given in the GDPR.
"**Data Subjects**" means the natural persons to whom the Personal Data
relates, primarily the Controller's students, employees, and platform users.

## 4. Nature and purpose of processing

The Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller solely for
the following purposes:

a) Operating the InTransparency platform (account management, profile
   matching, messaging, application tracking, placement reporting);

b) Providing AI-assisted matching between students and opportunities, with
   per-match explainability records preserved per AI Act Art. 12;

c) Generating aggregated reports for the Controller (placement rates,
   employer engagement, skill gap analysis);

d) Securing the platform (authentication, fraud detection, audit logging,
   bot protection);

e) Complying with legal obligations applicable to the Processor.

The Processor will not process Personal Data for any other purpose without
prior written consent of the Controller.

---

## 5. Duration

This DPA enters into force on the date of signature and remains effective
for the duration of the Main Agreement, plus the retention periods required
by applicable law and as specified in *Annex III — Retention Schedule*.

## 6. Obligations of the Processor

The Processor undertakes to:

6.1 **Process Personal Data only on documented instructions** of the
Controller, including with regard to transfers of Personal Data to a third
country, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law to which the
Processor is subject.

6.2 **Ensure that persons authorised to process the Personal Data** have
committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate
statutory obligation of confidentiality.

6.3 **Take all measures required pursuant to Article 32 GDPR**, as detailed
in *Annex II — Technical and Organisational Measures*.

6.4 **Respect the conditions for engaging another processor** (sub-processor)
as set out in section 7 below.

6.5 **Assist the Controller, taking into account the nature of the
processing**, by appropriate technical and organisational measures, to
fulfil obligations to respond to requests for exercising Data Subject
rights laid down in Chapter III GDPR.

6.6 **Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance** with Articles 32 to 36
GDPR, taking into account the nature of processing and the information
available.

6.7 **At the choice of the Controller**, delete or return all Personal Data
after the end of the provision of services relating to processing, and
delete existing copies, unless Union or Member State law requires storage
of the Personal Data.

6.8 **Make available to the Controller all information necessary** to
demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in Article 28 GDPR
and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by
the Controller or another auditor mandated by the Controller.

6.9 **Immediately inform the Controller** if, in its opinion, an instruction
infringes the GDPR or other applicable Union or Member State data
protection provisions.

## 7. Sub-processors

7.1 The Controller authorises the Processor to engage the sub-processors
listed in *Annex I — List of Sub-processors* for the performance of the
services.

7.2 The Processor will inform the Controller of any intended changes
concerning the addition or replacement of sub-processors with at least
**30 days' notice**, thereby giving the Controller the opportunity to
object to such changes.

7.3 The Processor will impose on each sub-processor, by means of a written
contract, the same data protection obligations as set out in this DPA, in
particular providing sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate
technical and organisational measures.

7.4 The Processor remains fully liable to the Controller for the performance
of the sub-processor's obligations.

---

## 8. International transfers

8.1 Personal Data is primarily stored within the European Economic Area
(EEA).

8.2 Where transfer outside the EEA is necessary, the Processor relies on:

a) The EU-US Data Privacy Framework, where the recipient is certified;

b) Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2: controller-to-processor)
   adopted by Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914;

c) Supplementary technical measures: encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and
   at rest (AES-256), anonymisation of identifiers where feasible,
   contractual prohibitions on disclosing data to public authorities
   without prior notice (where legally permitted).

8.3 The current list of recipients outside the EEA, with applicable
safeguards, is set out in *Annex I*.

---

## 9. Security incidents

9.1 The Processor will notify the Controller **without undue delay and in
any case within 24 hours** of becoming aware of a Personal Data breach
affecting the Controller's data.

9.2 The notification will include, to the extent reasonably available:

a) The nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number
   of Data Subjects and records concerned;

b) The likely consequences;

c) The measures taken or proposed to mitigate the breach;

d) Contact details of the Processor's data protection contact.

9.3 The Processor will assist the Controller in fulfilling its obligation
to notify the supervisory authority and, where applicable, the Data
Subjects.

---

## 10. Audits

10.1 The Controller has the right to audit the Processor's compliance with
this DPA, either directly or through a third-party auditor bound by
confidentiality, **once per calendar year** and at the Controller's
expense.

10.2 The Processor may satisfy audit requests by providing recent
third-party security certifications (e.g., SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001) and
penetration test reports under NDA, subject to the Controller's reasonable
acceptance.

10.3 Audits requested following a Personal Data breach affecting the
Controller's data are not subject to the annual limit.

---

## 11. Data subject requests

11.1 The Processor will not respond to Data Subject requests directly except
on the documented instructions of the Controller, unless required by
applicable law.

11.2 The Processor provides self-service mechanisms for Data Subjects to
exercise their rights of access, rectification, erasure, and portability
within the platform itself. The Controller is informed of any erasure
request executed via these mechanisms within 7 days.

---

## 12. Term and termination

12.1 This DPA terminates upon termination of the Main Agreement.

12.2 Upon termination, the Processor will, at the Controller's choice
expressed within 30 days of termination:

a) Return all Personal Data to the Controller in a structured,
   machine-readable format; or

b) Delete all Personal Data, except where Union or Member State law
   requires retention.

12.3 The Processor will provide written certification of deletion within
60 days of termination, unless retention is legally required.

---

## 13. Liability

The liability of the Parties under this DPA is governed by the limitation
of liability clauses of the Main Agreement, except for liabilities arising
from infringement of GDPR which cannot be limited under applicable law.

---

## 14. Governing law and jurisdiction

This DPA is governed by Italian law. Any dispute arising out of or in
connection with this DPA will be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of
the competent courts of *[City — typically Milan]*.

# Annex II — Technical and Organisational Measures

The Processor implements the following measures, evaluated against
Article 32 GDPR and the state of the art:

## Encryption

- **At rest**: AES-256, AWS-KMS managed keys, applied at the database
  layer (Neon Postgres) and at the object storage layer (Cloudflare R2).
- **In transit**: TLS 1.2+ enforced server-side; HSTS with
  `max-age=31536000; includeSubDomains` on the canonical domain.
- **Application-level**: AES-256-GCM encryption of TOTP secrets at rest
  (key custody: Vercel environment variables, separate from database
  credentials). Bcrypt cost-10 hashing for passwords and MFA backup
  codes.

## Access control

- Role-based access control (RBAC) at the application layer with five
  segments (Student, Recruiter, University, Professor, Tech Park) plus
  platform Admin.
- Least-privilege principle for staff: no production database read
  access for non-engineering roles; engineering access logged.
- Two-factor authentication (TOTP) available for all user accounts;
  required for platform administrators.

## Logging and monitoring

- Per-event audit log (`AuditLog` table) records sensitive actions
  including data access by non-subjects, deletions, AI matching events,
  authentication events, and administrative actions. Retention: 7 years.
- Per-user audit log surface available to Data Subjects in their
  privacy dashboard.
- Behavior analytics (page views, clicks) only collected with explicit
  user consent; IP anonymised at storage time.

## Resilience and recovery

- Daily automated backups of the primary database with 7-day
  point-in-time recovery via Neon native tooling.
- Vercel platform provides multi-region failover for the application
  tier.
- Documented incident response procedure with 24-hour breach
  notification to controllers.

## Network and application security

- Web Application Firewall and DDoS mitigation at the edge (Vercel +
  Cloudflare).
- Content Security Policy, X-Frame-Options DENY, X-Content-Type-Options
  nosniff, SameSite=Lax cookies.
- Cloudflare Turnstile bot protection on registration, login, and
  high-value public endpoints.
- Per-IP rate limiting on authentication, AI, file upload, and public
  read endpoints.
- Static analysis and dependency vulnerability scanning in CI.

## Personnel

- All staff with access to production systems sign a confidentiality
  agreement and complete privacy training annually.
- Contractor access is time-bound and requires explicit ticket-driven
  authorisation.
